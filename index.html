Why this exists

Belgrade has no independent, transparent price index. English-language coverage of the region's capital markets barely exists at all.

Local portals show asking prices one listing at a time. Government registries publish transaction data with a long lag. Nobody aggregates either into something an investor, analyst, or diaspora buyer can actually use — and nobody is writing English-language markets commentary on the region with the rigor it deserves. Terazije Index and the Balkan Markets Brief exist to close both gaps, together.

Methodology

Built to be checked, not just trusted.

This is an asking-price index, stated plainly. It measures what sellers list for, not what buyers ultimately pay. That distinction matters, and we keep it visible rather than smoothing it over.

  1. Collection

    Listings gathered monthly from Serbia's major property portals, throttled and off-peak, aggregate statistics only — no individual listings, photos, or contact details are ever republished.

  2. Cleaning

    Cross-posted and duplicate listings removed, statistical outliers excluded, all prices normalized to €/m² using declared floor area.

  3. Aggregation

    Median and mean €/m² calculated per municipality, alongside month-on-month and year-on-year change and listing volume as a demand proxy.

  4. Calibration

    Checked periodically against RGZ (Republički geodetski zavod) transaction-price data where available, to surface — not hide — the gap between asking and closing prices.

Disclosure: the operator of Terazije Index holds property interests in the wider region. This is disclosed on principle, not because it affects the data — the methodology above is mechanical and applied uniformly regardless of any personal holding.
